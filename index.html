About Me

The background above is a photo I took  of a friend backcountry snowboarding near the Cordón Caulle volcanic eruption three hours drive from my hometown of Puerto Montt, Chile. That was in July 2011, a few years after graduating with an economics degree. I've since moved to Chicago to experience living in a large U.S. city while working at a non-profit (the YMCA), eventually joining and graduating from a web development boot camp (DBC) before moving to Washington, D.C. to work at a rapidly growing tech company (Optoro) in late 2016.

Optoro has been an ideal place for me to grow as a software engineer, and as a person, while simultaneously adding value. I've worked with little direction while collaborating with the company's tech leaders to improve the company we all care about. I've taken on challenging projects like increasing our product's site reliability via implementing an effective postmortem process, streamlining business to tech team issue resolution, to playing a major role in building out successful new teams.

Since early 2019, I've been working remotely from Chicago, currently in the Warehouse Tools team, programming within a monolithic repo as well as a services ecosystem mostly in Ruby, Rails, Haml, Javascript, AngularJS, VueJS, and all the necessary ops/infrastructure tools. Being in an agile team focused on delivering timely results, I shift my focus to alleviating whatever bottlenecks we encounter — including performing quality engineering duties, unblocking teammates via pair programming, resolving high impact outages while being on-call, flagging process issues and working to improve our team, or really whatever is needed to get the job done.

All of my Optoro code commits are on Gitlab, behind private repositories whose code cannot be shared (though I'm happy to talk about what I've built). For more details on the things I've worked on, here's my GitHub, my resume, and my LinkedIn account.
